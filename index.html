📊 Escalation Breakdown

Design duration is required to calculate escalation. Please enter Design Duration in the Cost Calculation Parameters section (Budget step), then open this breakdown again.

Compounded Escalation Formula

Each year's escalation is calculated as: Base Cost × ((1 + Rate)Effective Years - 1)
Year 1 has no escalation (baseline year) unless NTP is after the March 1 following today's date.
Subsequent years compound from the baseline, e.g., Year 2 = 1 year of escalation, Year 3 = 2 years compounded, etc.

Note: Year 1 has no escalation unless NTP is after March 1 of the following fiscal year. Financial escalation year starts March 1.

KIE Labor Cost Distribution (S-Curve)

S-Curve: Logistic sigmoid S(t) = 1 / (1 + e-k(t - tmid)), where k = 10/N (N = duration in months, tmid = N/2). Produces a standard design engineering distribution: ~4% cumulative at 20% of duration, ~50% at midpoint, ~96% at 80%. Monthly cost = incremental S-curve difference, normalized so total = 100%. Escalation is compounded annually at the base rate, applied per the March 1 fiscal year boundary.
